KPI 1
Family Preservation Rate
On Track ✓
Children Preserved ÷ Children with Valid 3-Mo FWA
50 of 58 eligible children preserved  ·  20 children excluded (no valid FWA)
Data integrity notes
What we count
Each child belonging to an active mom counts as one data point, not the mom herself
Preserved means
Children whose Family Preservation Impact falls in the Prevention %: Prevented from CPS Involvement (stayed home, no CW contact), Prevented from Foster Care Placement (had CW involvement but stayed home), or Prevented from Permanent Removal (was in foster care but reunified)
Not preserved
Children in Temporary Removal (currently in foster care/kinship) or Permanent Removal (will not return). These are the Out of Care % and are excluded from the numerator
Who's included
Children whose moms had a completed Family Wellbeing Assessment within the last 3 months as of March 31
Who's not included
Children whose moms don't yet have a current assessment on file. They show up in the FWA Data Integrity tab so coordinators can follow up
Why 3 months?
ĒMA launched its new system in December 2025. There isn't yet enough history to use a 6-month window. This will expand in future quarters.
⚠ Known limitation
A small edit to any part of an assessment updates the assessment date, so "recent" doesn't always mean a full re-assessment was done. Coordinators should confirm full re-assessments were completed, not just edits.

KPI 3
Learning Progress
On Track ✓
Completions where post composite > pre composite ÷ Completions with valid pre & post pair
13 of 18 eligible completions show improvement  ·  NPP: 8/10 · EP: 3/5 · RR: 2/3  ·  excludes missing or inverted score pairs
Data integrity notes
What counts as improvement
Any increase in a mom's total score between her start-of-track assessment and her end-of-track assessment - even one point up counts
Who's included
Moms who completed a track in Q1 and have both a start and end assessment on file - each track counts separately
Who's not included
Moms with a missing start or end assessment, and a small number of records with data entry issues where the end date was recorded before the start date - all excluded counts are shown alongside this KPI
Each track is its own score
NPP, EP, and RR each use their own assessment - a mom completing two tracks counts twice, once per track
Not related to FWA
This KPI uses track learning assessments only - completely separate from the Family Wellbeing Assessment used in KPI 1 and KPI 2

Data integrity notes
Unique counts
Each advocate appears in exactly one bucket - priority order is: Group facilitator → Paired 1:1 → Waiting to be Paired → Taking a Break
Pairing source
Group facilitator and Paired 1:1 counts are sourced from mom's pairing record - this is the truth for whether pairing happened, regardless of what the advocate's own status says
Sub-status fallback
Waiting to be Paired and Taking a Break are sourced from the advocate's own sub-status - only used when no active pairing is found on mom's side
⚠ Known workaround
Some advocates are set to Active status with a Taking a Break sub-status as a system workaround. Mom's pairing record takes precedence - if a pairing exists on mom's side they are correctly classified as Paired 1:1 or Group. Only if no pairing exists do they fall into the Taking a Break bucket.

Multiple counts
One advocate may appear in multiple Q1 Activity counts - an advocate who trained and was approved in Q1 appears in both. These are independent events, not a funnel.

Data Integrity Notes
How conversion is counted
Converted = referral status is "Engaged In Program"
Pending
Status is "Prospect" or "Prospect Intake Scheduled" - appointment may still happen. Not a failed referral.
Did not convert
Status is "Did Not Engage In Program" - includes: unable to contact, client declined, ineligible, duplicate referral, or referred to another program. These are closed and excluded from the pending count.
Denominator
Conversion rate = Engaged In Program ÷ all referrals received (excluding duplicates). Pending referrals are shown separately - not included in the rate.
